# Meeting Transcription: How to Record and Transcribe Meetings in Real Time

## What Is Meeting Transcription?

Meeting transcription is the process of converting spoken conversations from a meeting, conference, interview, lecture, or discussion into written text.

A meeting transcription tool uses speech recognition and artificial intelligence to automatically convert audio into a searchable and editable transcript.

Instead of manually taking notes while listening to a meeting, users can record the conversation and let AI generate the transcript automatically.

Meeting transcription is commonly used for:

* Business meetings
* Team meetings
* Client meetings
* Interviews
* Lectures
* Conferences
* Seminars
* Research
* Podcasts
* Training sessions
* Medical discussions
* Legal meetings

---

## How Does AI Meeting Transcription Work?

An AI meeting transcription system typically follows several steps:

1. Capture audio from the meeting.
2. Process the recorded speech.
3. Recognize spoken words using speech recognition technology.
4. Convert speech into text.
5. Identify different speakers when speaker recognition is available.
6. Remove unnecessary filler words and repeated phrases.
7. Organize the transcript into readable text.
8. Allow users to search, edit, export, and share the transcript.

MeetingMinutes combines these capabilities into a mobile meeting recording and transcription workflow.

The basic process is:

**Record → Transcribe → Identify Speakers → Clean Transcript → Search → Edit → Export → Share**

# Meeting Transcription and Translation

Transcription and translation are two different processes.

**Translation** converts text from one language into another.

The translation system supports:

* Bidirectional translation

# How to Choose a Meeting Transcription App

When choosing a meeting transcription tool, consider:

### 1. Transcription Accuracy

Check how accurately the tool handles your language, accent, and recording environment.

### 2. Real-Time Transcription

If you need to review text while a meeting is happening, look for real-time transcription.

### 3. Speaker Recognition

For meetings with multiple participants, speaker identification can make transcripts easier to understand.

### 4. Language Support

International users should check whether the application supports the required languages and dialects.

### 5. Offline Recording

If you frequently attend meetings in locations with unreliable internet access, offline recording can be important.

### 6. AI Meeting Summaries

Look for tools that can turn long transcripts into structured summaries and meeting minutes.

### 7. Export and Sharing

Check whether transcripts and meeting notes can be exported and shared in the formats you need.

### 8. Search and Organization

Keyword search and date-based organization make it easier to retrieve information from past meetings.

### 9. Cross-Device Synchronization

Cloud synchronization can make meeting content accessible across multiple devices.
