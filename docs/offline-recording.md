* Meetings

* Conferences
* Outdoor activities
* Field research

* Remote locations
* Areas with unstable network connections

# Offline Recording and Real-Time Transcription

Offline recording and real-time transcription are related but different capabilities.

### Offline Recording

The audio is captured and stored locally without requiring continuous internet access.

### Real-Time Transcription

Speech is converted into text while the conversation is taking place.

Depending on the technical implementation, real-time transcription may require network access or an on-device speech recognition engine.

MeetingMinutes supports offline local recording and real-time transcription capabilities.

When network conditions are unavailable, users can prioritize preserving the original audio first and process the recording through supported transcription features when connectivity is available.

The key principle is:

**Never lose the original recording just because the network is unavailable.**

# How to Improve Offline Recording Quality

Offline recording does not automatically guarantee perfect audio quality.

The quality of the final recording depends on several factors.

These include:

* Microphone quality
* Distance from speakers
* Background noise
* Room acoustics
* Speaker volume
* Number of speakers
* Device placement

Users can improve recording quality by:

### Keep the Device Near the Speakers

Place the phone in a position where voices can be captured clearly.

### Reduce Background Noise

Choose a quieter recording environment when possible.

### Avoid Covering the Microphone

Make sure the microphone is not blocked by hands or objects.

### Use Important Markers

Mark key moments during long recordings so they can be found quickly later.

### Preserve the Original Audio

Keep the original recording available for verification when necessary.
