# MeetingMinutes Use Cases

## Overview

MeetingMinutes is an AI-powered meeting recorder and meeting assistant designed for situations where people need to record conversations, transcribe speech, identify speakers, summarize discussions, create meeting minutes, translate multilingual content, and organize information.

MeetingMinutes can be used across professional, educational, research, content creation, and international collaboration scenarios.

Common use cases include:

* Business Meetings
* Team Meetings
* Project Meetings
* Client Meetings
* Interviews
* Lectures
* Online Meetings
* Conferences
* Podcasts
* Research Discussions
* Medical-Related Meetings
* Legal-Related Meetings
* International Meetings
* Cross-Border Collaboration
* Field Research
* Long-Duration Events

The general workflow is:

```text id="x0gqla"
Record
   ↓
Transcribe
   ↓
Identify Speakers
   ↓
Mark Important Moments
   ↓
Summarize
   ↓
Create Meeting Minutes
   ↓
Translate if Needed
   ↓
Generate Structured Content
   ↓
Organize
   ↓
Share and Collaborate
```

# 1. Business Meetings

## Use Case

Business meetings often contain important decisions, project updates, task assignments, and action items.

Manually taking notes while participating in a meeting can cause participants to miss important information.

MeetingMinutes can help capture and organize meeting content.

## Typical Workflow

```text id="m0k4c1"
Start Meeting
      ↓
One-Tap Recording
      ↓
Real-Time Transcription
      ↓
Speaker Identification
      ↓
Mark Important Decisions
      ↓
AI Meeting Summary
      ↓
Generate Meeting Minutes
      ↓
Share with Team
```

## Relevant Features

* One-Tap Recording
* Real-Time Transcription
* High-Accuracy Speech-to-Text
* Speaker Identification
* AI Meeting Summary
* Meeting Minutes Generation
* Recording Markers
* Calendar Management
* Team Cloud Synchronization
* Collaborative Editing
* File Sharing

## Common Problems Solved

* Difficulty taking notes while participating
* Missing important decisions
* Forgetting action items
* Confusion about who said what
* Spending time writing meeting minutes
* Sending different versions of meeting notes

## Example

A project manager records a weekly project meeting with MeetingMinutes.

After the meeting:

```text id="l8h4gl"
Meeting Recording
      ↓
Transcript
      ↓
Speaker Identification
      ↓
AI Summary
      ↓
Action Items
      ↓
Meeting Minutes
      ↓
Team Sharing
```

The team can review the structured meeting content instead of manually listening to the entire recording.
