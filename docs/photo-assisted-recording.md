# Photo-Assisted Recording: Capture Photos and Audio Together with MeetingMinutes

## What Is Photo-Assisted Recording?

Photo-assisted recording combines audio recording with photos to create a more complete record of a meeting, lecture, interview, workshop, or event.

Audio can capture what people say, but it cannot always capture important visual information.

For example, a meeting may include:

* Whiteboard notes
* Presentation slides
* Handwritten notes
* Documents
* Charts
* Diagrams
* Product prototypes
* On-site materials

MeetingMinutes supports photo-assisted recording, allowing users to take photos while recording audio and associate the captured images with the corresponding point in the recording.

This helps users preserve both spoken and visual information in a single recording workflow.

---

# Why Use Photo-Assisted Recording?

Traditional audio recording only captures sound.

However, many meetings and events contain important information that is visual rather than verbal.

For example:

```text id="a8k4p2"
Meeting Discussion
        +
Whiteboard
        +
Presentation Slide
        +
Printed Document
```

If users only record the audio, they may lose important visual context.

Photo-assisted recording helps combine:

**Audio + Photos + Transcript + Meeting Notes**

This creates a more complete record of the event.

# How to Use Photo-Assisted Recording

## Step 1: Start a Recording

Open MeetingMinutes and start recording the meeting, lecture, interview, or event.

## Step 2: Continue Recording

Allow the audio recording to continue normally.

## Step 3: Identify Important Visual Information

Look for information that may be difficult to preserve through audio alone.

Examples include:

* Whiteboards
* Slides
* Documents
* Charts
* Handwritten notes

## Step 4: Take a Photo

Capture the visual information using the photo-assisted recording workflow.

## Step 5: Continue Recording

Return to the conversation without stopping the main recording.

## Step 6: Add a Recording Marker if Needed

Mark the moment if the visual information is particularly important.

## Step 7: Finish the Recording

Stop the recording when the meeting or event ends.

## Step 8: Review the Recording

Review available:

* Audio
* Photos
* Transcript
* Recording markers
* Meeting notes

## Step 9: Generate an AI Summary

Use supported AI features to extract key information.

## Step 10: Create Meeting Minutes

Organize the meeting into structured documentation.
