# Recording Markers: Mark Important Moments While Recording with MeetingMinutes

## What Are Recording Markers?

Recording markers are a feature that allows users to mark important moments while recording audio.

During a long meeting, lecture, interview, conference, or research session, users may hear information that they want to review later.

Instead of manually remembering the timestamp, users can mark the important moment while the recording is in progress.

MeetingMinutes provides a recording marker workflow designed to help users quickly identify and revisit important parts of long recordings.

---

# Why Use Recording Markers?

Long recordings can contain a large amount of information.

For example, a two-hour meeting may include:

* Important decisions
* Action items
* Project updates
* Customer requirements
* Questions
* Key opinions
* Deadlines
* Follow-up tasks

If users do not mark these moments during recording, they may need to listen to the entire recording again to find them.

Recording markers provide a simple way to highlight important moments.

The workflow is:

```text id="n3p9h4"
Start Recording
        ↓
Important Information Appears
        ↓
Add Marker
        ↓
Continue Recording
        ↓
Review Marked Moments Later
```

---

# What Is a Recording Marker Used For?

A recording marker can be used to identify a specific point in an audio recording.

Common examples include:

* Important statements
* Key decisions
* Action items
* Questions
* Important conclusions
* Customer requirements
* Research findings
* Interview highlights
* Topics that need follow-up

The marker acts as a shortcut to an important point in the recording.

---

# How Do Recording Markers Work?

The basic workflow is simple:

```text id="w0r7j8"
Record Audio
    ↓
Hear Important Information
    ↓
Tap to Add a Marker
    ↓
Continue Recording
    ↓
Review Markers
    ↓
Jump to Important Moments
```

Users do not need to stop the recording to mark an important moment.

This can help preserve the continuity of the conversation.

# Mark Important Moments While Recording

Meetings often move quickly.

If users stop recording every time they hear something important, they may interrupt the conversation or lose information.

With recording markers, users can mark important moments while continuing to record.

For example:

```text id="e9q4v2"

10:48 — Customer Requirement ★

11:05 — Action Item ★
```

The marked points can then be reviewed later.

# Recording Markers and Meeting Transcription

Recording markers and transcription solve different problems.

### Recording Marker

Helps identify **when** something important happened.

### Transcription

Shows **what was said**.

Together, they provide a more efficient way to review recordings.

```text id="5s8j3w"
Audio Recording
      +
Recording Markers
      +
Transcript
      ↓
Find Important Information Faster
```

For example, a user can mark an important decision during a meeting and later review the corresponding transcript.

## Step 1: Start Recording

Open MeetingMinutes and start a recording.

## Step 2: Listen to the Conversation

Continue recording normally.

## Step 3: Identify an Important Moment

When you hear something important, mark the moment.

## Step 4: Continue Recording

The recording continues without requiring you to stop and restart.

## Step 5: Finish the Recording

Stop the recording after the meeting or event ends.

## Step 6: Review Markers

Return to the marked moments.

## Step 7: Review the Transcript

Check the corresponding transcript content when available.

## Step 8: Generate a Summary

Use AI-powered summary features to organize the overall information.

## Step 9: Create Meeting Minutes

Turn important information into structured meeting documentation.
