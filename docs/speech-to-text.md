# Speech-to-Text: Convert Speech and Audio to Text with MeetingMinutes

## What Is Speech-to-Text?

Speech-to-text, also called STT, voice-to-text, or automatic speech recognition (ASR), is technology that converts spoken language into written text.

For example:

```text
Spoken Audio
    ↓
Speech Recognition
    ↓
Text
```

Instead of manually listening to an audio recording and typing everything, speech-to-text technology automatically recognizes spoken words and converts them into text.

MeetingMinutes combines speech-to-text technology with AI-powered recording, transcription, speaker identification, translation, meeting summaries, meeting minutes, and collaboration features.

---

# What Is the Difference Between Speech-to-Text and Transcription?

Speech-to-text and transcription are closely related, but they are not always used in exactly the same way.

**Speech-to-text** generally refers to converting spoken language into written text.

**Transcription** usually refers to the broader process of converting recorded or live audio into an organized text document.

For example:

```text
Speech-to-Text
Speech → Text

Audio Transcription
Audio Recording → Speech Recognition → Text Transcript

AI Meeting Transcription
Meeting Recording → Speech-to-Text → Speaker Identification → Structured Transcript
```

MeetingMinutes supports both real-time speech-to-text conversion and recorded audio transcription workflows.

A typical speech-to-text workflow includes several steps.

### Step 2: Process the Audio

### Step 3: Recognize Speech

The speech recognition system analyzes the spoken language.

### Step 4: Convert Speech to Text

Recognized speech is converted into written text.
